Prohibited use of a vehicle

Last Revision document date: 11.01.2024

In the event that you commit prohibited acts with a vehicle booked through Rentality, we may take the following measures:

- temporary suspension of the user's activities on the Rentality platform;

- blocking the user's account on the Rentality platform;

- require you to pay fines.

The following activities and use of vehicles are prohibited under the Rentality Platform Terms of service:

1. Putting a person other than the Primary Driver in control of the vehicle. Only drivers who have been approved by Rentality may book and drive the vehicle on a Rentality trip. Guests may request and receive approval to add an additional Secondary Driver to the trip who must be listed on the rental agreement as an additional driver. Primary responsibility rests with the primary guest, the Primary Driver, who is registered on the platform and makes the trip reservation on the Rentality platform.

2. Handing over the driving of the vehicle to a person who does not have a driver's license or who does not have sufficient driving experience as specified in the Terms of service.

3. Towing the rented car in any way, towing any kind of trailer without the host's approval.

4. Driving a manual transmission vehicle without sufficient skill in the use of the clutch and manual transmission. Damage to the manual transmission is the responsibility of the primary driver.

5. Smoking any type of cigarette in the vehicle, including all types of electronic cigarettes and vapes.

6. Refueling the vehicle with a type of fuel that does not meet the specifications for the vehicle.

7. Leaving the vehicle unattended while it is running or with keys inside.

8. Exceeding the authorized speed limit.

9. Participating in any racing, competition in automobiles.

10. Intentional or reckless disregard for safety.

11. Transporting persons or property for a fee, providing taxi cab service or delivery service.

12. Using a vehicle outside of the travel time specified in the platform order.

13. Using a vehicle while committing a crime or any other illegal activity.

14. Driving under the influence of alcohol, medication or other substances that are prohibited or not recommended while operating a motor vehicle.

15. Carrying more passengers than the seat belts allow.

16. Carrying cargo, luggage, or other items in excess of the rated load capacity of the vehicle.

17. It is prohibited to use mobile communication devices while driving as they can distract you from the road, this includes texting, checking email, and using a cell phone without a hands-free device.

18. Leaving the country in which the car has been booked.

19. Carrying dangerous or any illegal substances in the car.

20. Transporting a pet without prior authorization from the owner of the car.

21. Making alterations, additions or improvements to a motor vehicle.

22. Spreading fraudulent messages about the vehicle or the owner.

The prohibited actions listed are not an exhaustive list. Any improper or inappropriate use of the vehicle may be considered prohibited.
